About the Beautiful Islands of Hawaii

Hawaiian Wildlife

Among the various symbols that hold great significance in Hawaiian culture, the Honu, or Hawaiian green turtle, stands as a powerful representation of resilience, wisdom, and connection to the natural world. One of the Earth’s oldest animals, the honu is known for guiding the Hawaiian people to the Islands.There are many encounters that you will come across with the wildlife in Hawaii. One being the Honu. Honu means turtle, and these beautiful creatures are not to be messed with. There are many laws in place to protect the Honu, as well as the people of the island coming together to always remind others to stay far from the wildlife and never disturb its habitat. It is beautiful to see the people protecting the island.

Hawaiian Hibiscus

The Hibiscus flower is known around the island for its size, and the colors that it comes in. There are yellow, red, and pink Hibiscus around the island. The yellow Hibiscus is the state flower.In addition to its aesthetic charm, hibiscus flowers have been utilized for centuries for their potential health benefits, particularly in addressing various minor ailments. The petals and extracts of the hibiscus plant have been traditionally employed to alleviate stomach issues, stimulate appetite, and provide relief from common cold symptoms. Furthermore, the hibiscus is known for its anti-inflammatory properties, which may aid in reducing swelling and discomfort. Studies have also suggested that hibiscus consumption could contribute to maintaining healthy blood pressure levels.

Keep the Beaches Clean

Any beach that you come across on the island is breathtaking, and it is our job to keep it that way. The island has a fragile eco system, it's beauty isn’t just for show, it is a vital part of the state’s identity, economy, and way of life. Cleanliness plays a crucial role in keeping Hawaii the paradise we all know and love. When the land, ocean, and public spaces are clean, it doesn’t just look good; it preserves the delicate balance of the ecosystem, supports local communities, and respects the cultural traditions of caring for the land.
